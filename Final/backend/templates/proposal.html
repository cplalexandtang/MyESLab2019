Statistical Machine Learning Final Project

This is the proposal of the final project of the course: Statistical Machine Learning

Predicting Scores of MLB Games

Dataset

The MLB-ELO Dataset (GitHub Link).

Methods to be Used

  • Supervised Learning
    • Regression via Gradient Descent Methods (e.g. SGD, Adam)
    • Regression with Regularization
    • Experimental: Deep Neural Network tricks (e.g. Dropout)
  • Unsupervised Learning
    • PCA on Row/Column Vectors of the Obtained Latent Matrix
    • Clustering on Vectors after Dimension Reduction
    • t-SNE for Visualization of Latent Vectors

Explaining Latent Matrix

Having obtained A and H, it is possible for us to predict the results of future games and evaluate its performance. A plot of time verses error will be given to conclude that until what time does such prediction model gather enough information to perform well enough, since such matrix factorization should be executed regularly during the whole Regular Season.

Moreover, by analyzing latent matrix A and H, it enables us to explain the extant difference of performance while being a host or an away. Some trends can also be shown by tracing each team’s latent vector with respect to time, which may also imply the evolution of players’ morale for the time being.
